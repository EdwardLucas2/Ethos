# Ethos: The Comprehensive Contract Overview (The "Hub") This document defines the architecture, user stories, and UI components for the central "Contract Overview" screen. This screen serves as the operational heart of a specific contract, managing current progress, historical resolution, and collective accountability. --- ## 1. Core Functions & Interactions The Contract Overview is not just a dashboard; it is a high-stakes command center. It must support: * **Parallel Cycle Management:** Seamlessly toggling between the *Active Cycle* (uploading new proof) and the *Previous Cycle* (if it is still in "Pending Resolution"). * **Social Accountability:** Viewing exactly who is pulling their weight and who is slacking in real-time. * **Verification Bridge:** Acting as the primary entry point for reviewing friends' evidence to "unlock" progress. * **Stakes Awareness:** Keeping the "Forfeit" top-of-mind to maintain the competitive tension. --- ## 2. User Stories ### The Active Committer * "As a participant, I want to see a clear 'Snap Proof' button so I can immediately document my habit completion as soon as I finish it." * "As a participant, I want to see my progress bar divided into 'Verified' vs. 'Pending' so I can see that my work is done even if my friends haven't voted yet." * "As a participant, I want to see a countdown to the end of the period so I know exactly how much time I have to hit my goal." ### The Group Enforcer (The Voter) * "As a group member, I want to be alerted if there is evidence I haven't reviewed yet so I don't hold up the final resolution." * "As a group member, I want to see a feed of all recent activity so I can stay motivated by seeing my friends' progress." ### The Cycle Manager * "As a user, I want to toggle back to 'Last Week' to see who ended up winning/losing while I continue working on 'This Week's' goals." * "As a winner, I want to see a clear 'Settle Up' button once a cycle is resolved so I can confirm the debt has been paid." --- ## 3. Screen Sections & UI Anatomy ### A. The Period Switcher (The "Cycle Toggle") * **Description:** A segmented control at the top of the content area. * **States:** [CURRENT PERIOD] (Active) and [LAST PERIOD] (Resolving/Resolved). * **Function:** Switches the entire view's data between the active game and the recently completed one. ### B. The "Progress Arena" (League Table) * **Description:** A stack of chunky horizontal progress bars, one for each participant (up to 10). * **Visuals:** * **Solid Fill (e.g., Yellow):** Verified evidence. * **Patterned/Light Fill:** Pending evidence (uploaded but not yet voted on). * **Empty:** Remaining actions needed. * **Data Labels:** Participant Name, Habit Name (e.g., "Gym 3x"), and a fractional status (e.g., "2/3"). ### C. The Stake & Timeline Block * **Description:** A high-contrast card summarizing the "Rules of Engagement." * **Content:** * **The Forfeit:** "ON THE LINE: A PINT" with a skull/prize icon. * **The Clock:** A linear timeline showing Start Date, "Today" marker, and End Date. * **Style:** Bold 3px borders, hard shadows, massive typography. ### D. Primary Action Zone (Dynamic CTA) * **Description:** A large, persistent button area that changes based on user needs. * **States:** * **Action Needed:** "SNAP PROOF" (Camera icon). * **Review Needed:** "ALEX SUBMITTED PROOF. VERIFY IT" (Blue background, high urgency). * **Waiting:** "ALL CAUGHT UP. WAITING ON OTHERS." (Muted state). ### E. The Evidence Feed (The "Proof Wall") * **Description:** A 2-column masonry grid of the most recent photo uploads. * **Interaction:** Tapping a photo opens the **Evidence Approval** screen or a detailed view. * **Metadata:** Timestamp (e.g., "MON 7AM") and a small indicator of who uploaded it. * **Link:** A "VIEW ALL EVIDENCE" button that navigates to the dedicated **Evidence Review** page. ### F. Renewal & Exit (Footer Actions) * **Description:** Small, secondary links at the very bottom. * **Actions:** "Adjust Commitment" or "Opt-Out of Next Cycle." --- ## 4. Visual Language Requirements * **Style:** Neo-brutalist. Everything must feel "heavy" and intentional. * **Contrast:** Use the blue/yellow/red accent colors to distinguish between "Success," "Pending," and "Alert" states. * **Borders:** Consistent 3px black borders on every card and button.
