# Ethos: Contract Mechanics & Game Systems This document defines the core logic, scoring, and resolution systems for the Ethos MVP. --- ## 1. Participation & Structure * **Capacity:** 2 to 10 participants per contract. * **The Forfeit:** A single, shared penalty/reward set at creation (e.g., "A Pint", "$20"). It is the same for everyone in the contract. * **Time Periods:** Unified duration for all participants. * Weekly * Bi-weekly (14 days) * Monthly (Calendar month) * **Scheduling:** Customizable start date (Today or a specific future date). ## 2. Commitments & Progress * **Individual Goals:** Each participant can set a unique commitment and frequency within the shared time period (e.g., Alex does "Gym 3x/week" while Sarah does "Read 5x/week"). * **Multi-Tone Progress Bars:** * **Committed (Pending):** A lighter/patterned fill showing evidence has been uploaded but not yet verified. * **Verified (Locked):** A solid, high-contrast fill showing evidence has been approved by the group. * **Remaining:** The empty portion of the bar. ## 3. Evidence & Verification * **Majority Rule:** Evidence is approved or rejected based on a majority vote of the *other* participants. * **Self-Exclusion:** A user cannot vote on their own evidence. * **The "Verified Only" Rule:** Only verified evidence contributes to the final "Locked" progress bar and the win/loss determination. ## 4. Resolution Logic (The "End Game") At the end of the time period, the system evaluates the state of all progress bars: | Scenario | Outcome | | :--- | :--- | | **All Participants Full** | **Success:** No one owes anything. High-five animation. | | **Mixed Results (Some Full, Some Not)** | **Penalty:** Those who didn't finish owe the forfeit to those who did. Forfeits are distributed as evenly as possible. | | **Total Failure (No One Full)** | **Relative Penalty:** The person with the least progress owes the forfeit to the person with the most progress. | | **Draws** | **Neutral:** In the event of a tie (e.g., two people at 50% and no one finished), no one owes anything. | --- ## 5. User Stories for the Mechanic **The Committer** * "As a participant, I want to see exactly how many more 'Verified' actions I need before the period ends so I don't lose the bet." * "As a participant, I want to see my 'Pending' progress so I feel a sense of accomplishment while waiting for my friends to vote." **The Voter** * "As a group member, I want to be notified when a friend's progress depends on my vote so I can keep the game moving." * "As a group member, I want to see a clear summary of evidence before I cast a 'Reject' vote, as it has real financial/social consequences." **The Winner/Loser** * "As a winner, I want the app to clearly state exactly who owes me the forfeit so I don't have to do the math or confront them manually." * "As a loser, I want to see the 'Relative Failure' logic explained if no one won, so I understand why I'm the one paying up."
